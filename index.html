About Rebecca

Rebecca Elizabeth Schmidt is a first year student at the Lethbridge Polytechnic, focused and enamored with writing and coding. She often enjoys staying in and reading her Bible, writing blurbs for the characters she creates, drawing and crafting with her hands. That is why she enrolled into the multimedia production program, to take her characters and make them into comic characters for a published book. Her doll creation is a way to fully conceptualize her “Little Guys” and visualize them as a physical entity rather than a static drawing.

Rebecca didn’t enjoy public school much, her only solace was art class, even if the instructor openly disliked her and her faith. In that class, she was free to paint and draw as she wished, with little judgement for her inspiration. As she has grown in her faith, she is finding ways to put scripture into her writing and her characters, especially the books “psalms” and “Galatians”. Those books have spoken to her since her first year at Millar College of the Bible, where she kept learning more about herself and the Book she had learned about since she was little.

Her family has been nothing but a support for her, lovingly pointing out her shortcomings and raising her to be better, living out Galatians 6:1. She has one younger sister, and a close friend whom she calls her older brother. A few things have remained a constant in her life. Her faith, her drawing, and her love of music. Her love of music seems to be the most prominent, as she learned to sing at a very early age because of her Christian lifestyle and influence. Because of this, she was encouraged to pursue it in choir and by singing on her family’s worship team some Sundays. Within this bubble of song, she and her parents make a three-part choir. Rebecca as the Soprano, her mother as the alto/Melody, and her father as the bass. Rebecca’s mother often teases her about finding a tenor to complete the quartet.

Rebecca and her Art

Rebecca has been known for her creations all her life. Gifts at Christmas, her reputation at Church, and even in Bible College and her father’s Social circles. Whether it be through her drawings, her paintings, or her “Creepy” hobby of customizing dolls, she was never one to turn down an idea for a commission, character design, or novel plot. She, under the username “LyniriSpace” has been her father’s artist for twitch, and is known for her cute, cartoon-ish art style.
